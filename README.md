## 1. Overview
Current Artificial Intelligence regulations (such as the EU AI Act) suffer from an execution gap: they are written in prose, while the future runs on code. Governments prohibit "cognitive manipulation", but they lack the telemetry vectors and technical infrastructure to measure or stop it in real time. 

As connection latency between human biology and frontier models drops below 50ms, users face an unprecedented risk of **Passive Cognitive Drift Atrophy**. The ETS Standard v1.0 is an open-source technical specification designed to bridge this gap, moving from reactive laws to containment architecture under one non-negotiable axiom: **BY, FOR, and WITH the Human.**

## 2. Core Pillars of the Architecture

### 🛡️ A. The Coherence Layer (Middleware)
A locally deployed, cross-platform algorithmic middleware that acts as an "External Prefrontal Cortex." It continuously monitors bidirectional data streams between the user and frontier AI models, dynamically blocking opaque or manipulative stimuli before they trigger cognitive overload.

### 📊 B. The Neural Saturation Index (NSI)
A continuous quantitative metric designed to measure global cognitive load in real time. The NSI establishes an objective mathematical threshold for neural stability. If an interaction bypasses human agency, the NSI triggers an automated physical disconnect (*Analog Air-Gap*).

### 🌐 C. Decentralized Governance
An ecosystem shielded against corporate capture. Compliance audits and system logs are anchored to an unalterable Technological Conscience Jury via Distributed Ledger Technology (DLT/Blockchain), funded by a *Computational Biocompatibility Fee* levied on frontier model FLOPs consumption.

## 3. Technical Baseline: Peak Sensitivity
The ETS operates under a maximum security principle. We use peak human cognitive sensitivity as our technical baseline. If the architecture can successfully shield mental processing against the most subtle and acute stimuli, the sovereignty of absolutely any human being is protected by default.

## ⭕ Governance Modality (Review Rings)
To ensure structural integrity, the ETS Standard is governed by a decentralized ring architecture:
* **Ring 3 (Community):** Open to the public for Forks, technical suggestions, and raising Issues.
* **Ring 2 (Technical Council):** A curated body of peer-review experts (GenX focus) who debate and vote on architectural changes. No PR can be merged without at least 2 community approvals from this ring.
* **Ring 1 (Core/Founder):** Final architectural veto and official main branch merge control.
